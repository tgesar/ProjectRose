What to Know

Cultivate a mental health mantra with discrete skill sets. One might be: Learn from the past, live in the present, and plan for the future. Each of these ideas can be put into action.

Prioritize self-care. The sleep and exercise you get in a day has a tremendous impact on how you feel about yourself and your overall mental health.

Challenging negative thoughts as they arise is one of the best ways to cope with distress. Understanding basic CBT can help you recognize whether you are making a difficult situation feel even tougher.

Chances are you are harder on yourself than you are on others. Enter self-compassion. Think about how you would treat someone else if they felt the way you feel now. Why not extend that same kindness and understanding to yourself?

If you struggle with procrastination and setting goals, consider tackling and completing a small task, which will help you gain momentum toward other life changes.

Make Change Now

Track positive emotions. Recognizing these moments, ones that you experience dozens of times a day, will shift you from a mindset of getting by to one of flourishing and reveling in life’s small pleasures.

Take stock of the people in your life. If there’s someone with whom you have constant friction or whose behavior is unpredictable, consider whether you can minimize interaction. Lack of predictability in individuals can lead to higher levels of stress when engaging with them.

Assess your mood fluctuations. If you experience mood swings or an inability to regulate your emotions as well as you’d like to, you might benefit from consulting with a mental health professional.
